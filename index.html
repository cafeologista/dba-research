Research: AI & Kaizen in P&C Insurance

Doctoral study overview & participation details

Your organization is invited to participate in a research study being conducted for a dissertation at the Swiss School of Business and Management (SSBM).

About This Research

This doctoral study investigates how U.S.-based Property & Casualty insurance companies are integrating Artificial Intelligence into their operations, with a particular focus on:

  • Kaizen and continuous improvement methodologies
  • Leadership strategies for AI adoption
  • Employee engagement and training approaches
  • The intersection of AI technology with operational excellence practices

The research examines multiple P&C sectors including commercial lines, workers' compensation, and other insurance types to understand how AI is transforming workflows, decision-making, and organizational culture.

Research Goals:

  • Understand how leadership drives AI adoption in continuous improvement initiatives
  • Document employee experiences with AI tools and training programs
  • Identify best practices for integrating AI with Lean/Six Sigma/Kaizen methodologies
  • Examine the human dimension of AI transformation in insurance operations

Why Participate?

Your Insights Matter

Insurance professionals at all levels—from C-suite executives to frontline underwriters and claims adjusters—are experiencing AI transformation firsthand. Your perspective will help the industry understand:

  • What's actually working in AI implementation
  • Where challenges remain
  • How to support employees through technological change

    • Contribute to Industry Knowledge

    This research will advance understanding of AI transformation in P&C insurance and may inform future best practices for the sector.

Your participation helps create a roadmap for the future of AI integration in Property & Casualty insurance operations.

Participation Includes
  • SURVEY: Participants will be asked to complete a short online survey designed to gather insights into leadership strategies, organizational readiness, and continuous improvement practices in relation to artificial intelligence (AI) integration. The survey is administered through a secure, web-based platform and can be completed using any desktop or mobile device. It is estimated to take approximately 10--15 minutes to complete.
  • DOCUMENTATION (Optional): Organizations may also elect to provide relevant supporting documentation, such as process improvement reports, AI adoption frameworks, or leadership communication materials. These materials will be used solely for contextual analysis and will be handled in accordance with institutional research ethics and confidentiality standards.

You may participate in the survey without providing documentation. If you share documents, please remove sensitive details or provide redacted versions.

Who Should Participate

This study seeks input from three types of insurance professionals:


Leadership Roles (5 per organization)

  • C-suite executives (CTO, CIO, COO, Chief AI/Data Officers)
  • VPs and Directors involved in AI strategy and decision-making
  • Innovation and Transformation leaders

IT/Technical Roles (5 per organization)

  • AI/ML engineers and data scientists
  • Product managers and solution architects
  • IT managers and project leads working on AI implementation

Frontline/End-User Roles (5 per organization)

  • Underwriters using AI-powered tools
  • Claims adjusters and examiners
  • Innovation and Transformation leaders
  • Case managers and operational staff
