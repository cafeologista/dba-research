Research: AI & Kaizen in P&C Insurance

Doctoral study overview & participation details

About the Study

This doctoral research explores how Property & Casualty (P&C) insurance organizations integrate Artificial Intelligence (AI) into Kaizen-based continuous improvement practices. It examines leadership strategy, change management, communication, and cultural alignment through frameworks such as TOE, McKinsey 7-S, and ADKAR—aiming to identify practical approaches that support operational excellence.

Findings are used strictly for academic purposes and reported in aggregate. No individual or organization will be identified without explicit authorization.

Participation Includes

  • SURVEY: A short, confidential questionnaire for colleagues in Leadership, Underwriting, and IT involved in transformation initiatives.
  • DOCUMENTATION (Optional): Internal strategy-level documents your firm can share, such as AI adoption plans, process improvement frameworks, or change management materials. These may be anonymized or redacted. All shared materials remain confidential and are used strictly for academic purposes.

You may participate in the survey without providing documentation. If you share documents, please remove sensitive details or provide redacted versions.

Who Should Participate

Professionals within P&C insurance engaged in or overseeing transformation initiatives—especially where AI, analytics, underwriting, and operations intersect:

  • Leadership & Strategy (e.g., CIO/CTO, COO, Transformation/Innovation leads)
  • Underwriting & Product (e.g., heads of underwriting, practice leaders)
  • Technology & Data (e.g., enterprise applications, data science/ML, architecture)

Confidentiality & Data Handling

Survey responses are collected without personally identifying information and analyzed in aggregate. Optional documentation will be stored securely, treated as confidential, and used only for academic analysis within the scope of this research.

If you choose to share documentation, please remove or redact proprietary or personally identifiable information (PII). If needed, reference the Organizational Document Release Form to formalize permissions.

Survey Tool & Distribution

The survey is administered online and typically takes less than 10 minutes to complete. Invitation links are shared directly with eligible participants (e.g., via email or LinkedIn InMail) to help ensure that only intended audiences respond.
